# John Oliver on Police Gang Databases: “Get rid of them”  
*Guardian – TV & Radio*  

**Author:** Guardian staff  
**Published:** Mon 28 Jul 2025 16:46 BST (last modified 17:00 BST)  
**Topic:** Critique of U.S. law‑enforcement gang databases, their inaccuracies, racial bias, and impact on immigration.  

---  

## 1. Overview  

In the *Last Week Tonight* episode aired after a summer break, host **John Oliver** examined the United States’ reliance on “gang databases” – police‑maintained lists of individuals deemed gang‑affiliated. He argued that these lists are:

* **Too easy to be added to**,  
* **Extremely hard to be removed from**, and  
* **Capable of devastating personal consequences**, especially for people of colour and immigrants.  

Oliver’s main prescription: **abolish the databases** and replace them with genuine police work focused on actual violent activity.  

---  

## 2. Key Points from the Segment  

| Issue | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Definition** | “Basically lists the police keep of people they say are involved in gangs.” |
| **Lack of Oversight** | No external governmental or law‑enforcement review; inclusion criteria are police observations and “self‑admissions” (e.g., a social‑media post containing the word “gang”). |
| **Inconsistent & Opaque** | Reports describe databases as “notoriously inconsistent and opaque”, “riddled with questionable entries and errors”, and “rife with unreliable intelligence”. |
| **Racial Disparity** | Lists are overwhelmingly composed of people of colour. Example: Washington DC’s database had **only one white person**. |
| **Potential for Abuse** | In 2020 a Phoenix officer added **17 Black Lives Matter protesters** as “ACAB gang members” out of retaliation. |
| **Impact on Immigrants** | Designation as a gang member can: <br>• Deny bond or other relief. <br>• Make a person a higher priority for removal. <br>• Lead to detention and deportation without a criminal conviction. |
| **Oliver’s Verdict** | “Gang databases are way too easy to get on, way too hard to get off, and can turn people’s lives upside down.” → **Recommendation:** *Get rid of them.* |

## 3. Illustrative Case Studies  

### 3.1 Kilmar Ábrego García  

* **Background:** Salvadorian immigrant from Maryland, illegally deported by ICE under the Trump administration.  
* **Trigger:** In 2019 a Home Depot sighting led officers to file a report claiming he belonged to MS‑13 because he wore a **Chicago Bulls hat** and a hoodie with imagery they linked to “Hispanic gang culture”.  
* **Report Quote:** “Wearing the Chicago Bulls hat represents that they are in good standing with the MS‑13.” (Oliver’s satirical comment on “monthly dues”).  
* **Outcome:** Ábrego was denied bond, detained for **eight months**, and ultimately deported after ICE admitted an “administrative error”.  
* **Officer Fate:** The reporting officer was later suspended for unrelated misconduct and then fired.  

*Sources:* [Kilmar Ábrego García article]((https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/kilmar-abrego-garcia))  

### 3.2 Alex – Long Island, NY  

* **Background:** Hispanic teen (origin: Honduras).  
* **Database Entry:** School resource officer added him after he wore bright‑blue sneakers, said by security guards to be associated with MS‑13, and had “504” (Honduran country code) doodled on his backpack.  
* **Result:** ICE agents later arrested and deported him, citing the gang‑member designation despite no criminal conviction.  

### 3.3 Phoenix Protester Incident  

* **Background:** In 2020, a Phoenix police officer listed **17 Black Lives Matter protesters** as members of an “ACAB” gang.  
* **Motivation:** Retaliation against protest activity.  

---  

## 4. Police Rationale & Oliver’s Response  

* **Police Commissioner (Alex’s county)** – Stated that even low‑level “intelligence” of gang affiliation (not necessarily a crime) justifies partnering with ICE: “If we perceive someone as a public safety threat, we utilize all of our tools, which again includes immigration tools.”  
* **Oliver’s Counter:** “If someone is on your list of big‑bad criminals, and you can’t find any big‑bad crime to arrest them for, that suggests the issue might be your **fucking list**.”  

---  

## 5. Oliver’s Suggested Way Forward  

1. **Eliminate gang databases.**  
2. **Focus police resources on genuine violent activity** rather than label‑based lists.  
3. Recognise that **gang‑related violence is real**, but solutions must extend beyond policing and labeling.  

> “I’m not saying that violence associated with gangs isn’t real or isn’t a problem. I’m just saying the answer needs to go beyond policing and way beyond these databases.” – John Oliver

## 8. Quick Reference Summary  

* **Problem:** U.S. police gang databases are unregulated, biased, and often based on flimsy evidence (e.g., clothing, social‑media captions).  
* **Consequences:** Wrongful detentions, denials of bond, immigration raids, deportations, and disproportionate impact on people of colour.  
* **Evidence:** Cases of Kilmar Ábrego García, Alex (Long Island), Phoenix protester listings; multiple reports describing database flaws.  
* **Solution Proposed:** Abolish the databases; rely on traditional policing focused on actual violent conduct.
